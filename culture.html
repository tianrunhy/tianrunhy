Engineering with empathy. Building for the long, quiet after.

Mission & Vision

Why we exist — and where we're going.

Our mission is to make everyday tools that respect the user as a sovereign individual. Our vision is a digital life where "data stays local" is the default, not a feature.

Mission

Empower individual lives with offline-first, beautifully crafted apps.

Every Tianrun Hengyuan product exists to take a daily friction — getting lost on a trail, losing a receipt, breaking a streak — and turn it into a quiet, reliable, private experience.

Vision

A calmer, fairer mobile ecosystem for the next billion users.

We aim to ship one hundred serious, privacy-obsessed apps by 2030 — each able to operate on a phone in airplane mode and feel just as complete.

Our Values

Six principles we hold first.

When in doubt, we go back to these. They are how we hire, how we design, how we ship.

Privacy Sovereignty

If the user doesn't need a server, we don't ship a server. Data that doesn't leave the device can't be breached.

Craft Always Wins

We hold craft — typography, motion, copy, micro-interactions — to the same bar as engineering. Polish is a usability feature.

Honest Monetization

Ads and unlocks must feel respectful. Frequency caps, contextual placement, transparent labels. Never dark patterns.

Global from Day One

We design for translation, currency, accessibility, regional policy and connectivity edge cases before we ship the first build.

Curious Engineering

Every sprint reserves a few hours for research — edge ML, on-device LLMs, AR mapping. We explore so we ship smarter.

Long-term Stewardship

We keep apps supported for years, not quarters. Each release notes for old versions. We do not abandon users.

Transparent Documentation

We publish architecture diagrams, deletion procedures and threat models — a calm, readable version of "what we do with your data."

Compliance is a Feature

Audits, DPIAs, COPPA-gates, GDPR-K checks — we treat them as competitive advantages, not paperwork.

Leadership Principles

How we make decisions together

Even a small studio needs shared rails. These are the rules we use to disagree productively.

01 · Disagree then commit

Argue the data, then commit fully

We debate hard, document the decision, then unify behind it. Quiet-loud, not loud-loud.

02 · One team, one queue

Shared on-call, shared roadmap

Engineers, designers and PMs sit in the same Slack, share the same bugs, ship the same releases.

03 · Quality is a team sport

No "not my job" departments

Writing, QA, support, marketing — everyone can patch a UI typo or a backend trace.

04 · Boring technology on purpose

Choose what survives the long test of time

We pick proven, well-documented stacks over shiny ones — except in dedicated research sprints.
